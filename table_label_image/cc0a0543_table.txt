11 INCOME TAXES
Under the current laws of the Cayman Islands, Tarena International is not subject to tax on its income or capital gains. For the period from its inception on October 22,
2012 to December 31, 2022, Tarena HK did not have any assessable profits arising in or derived from HK SAR. Tarena International’s PRC subsidiaries and consolidated
VIEs and the subsidiaries of the VIEs file separate tax returns in the PRC. Effective from January 1, 2008, the PRC statutory income tax rate is 25% according to the
Corporate Income Tax (“CIT”) Law which was passed by the National People’s Congress on March 16, 2007.
Under the CIT Law, entities that qualify as “High and New Technology Enterprise” (“HNTE”) are entitled to a preferential income tax rate of 15%. In 2015, Tarena Tech
renewed its HNTE qualification, which entitled it to the preferential income tax rate of 15% from January 1, 2015 to December 31, 2017. In 2018, Tarena Tech renewed
its HNTE qualification, which entitled it to the preferential income tax rate of 15% from January 1, 2018 to December 31, 2020. In 2021, Tarena Tech renewed its HNTE
qualification, which entitled it to the preferential income tax rate of 15% from January 1, 2021 to December 31, 2023.
